## What this means for engineers

| Area | Impact & What to Prep |
|------|----------------------|
| **Powertrain / Energy** | • All major series are moving toward higher hybrid/ERS utilization. Expect **larger battery packs**, **faster thermal‑management loops**, and **more aggressive energy‑deploy strategies**. <br>• For F1, start modeling the 100 % synthetic‑fuel combustion cycle now – it changes fuel‑heat‑transfer coefficients and lambda control. |
| **Aerodynamics** | • F1’s rear‑wing redesign will shift the down‑force balance downstream. Prepare **CFD studies** on reduced rear‑wing element count (e.g., 2‑element vs 3‑element). <br>• IndyCar’s new tire profile calls for **re‑tuning suspension kinematics** to maintain optimal camber/load. |
| **Vehicle Dynamics** | • Hybrid‑assist in IndyCar ovals adds **torque vectoring** possibilities. Simulate **wheel‑slip control** under high‑speed banking. <br>• WEC’s AI‑driven BoP may result in frequent **balance‑of‑performance adjustments**; set up a **quick‑turn parameter sweep** for aero/engine maps. |
| **Data & Telemetry** | • More data streams (e.g., ERS state‑of‑charge, synthetic‑fuel sensor set) → need **higher‑bandwidth telemetry packages** (≥2 Gbps for F1). <br>• Implement **real‑time BoP feedback loops** for WEC/IMSA to adapt strategies on‑the‑fly. |
| **Compliance & Homologation** | • New fuel standards and hybrid caps have strict **regulatory reporting** deadlines (usually 90 days before season start). Keep documentation pipelines (CAD, test data) ready for **FIA/WSC/IMSA** submissions. |
