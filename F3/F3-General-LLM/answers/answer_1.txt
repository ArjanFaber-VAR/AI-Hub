### How to Find the Exact Figure for Your Car

1. **Check the latest technical regulation booklet** for the series you’re competing in. Most series publish a PDF on their official website (e.g., FIA‑F1.com, IndyCar.com, FIA‑WEC.com, etc.).
2. **Locate the “Minimum Weight” clause**—it’s usually in the “Vehicle Dimensions & Mass” or “Technical/Power‑Unit” chapter.
3. **Confirm what is included**: most series require the weight to be measured **with the driver, full fuel tank, fire‑suppression system, and all mandatory fluids**. Anything else (ballast, extra cooling, aerodynamic devices) is added **after** the minimum‑weight measurement.
4. **Take a “wet” measurement** (full fuel) unless the rulebook explicitly says “dry” (fuel‑free). Some series have separate “dry weight” for balance‑of‑performance calculations, but the competition minimum is always the “wet” figure.

### Quick Tips for Engineers

| Situation | What to do |
|-----------|------------|
| **You’re under the limit** | Add ballast in the prescribed locations (often front roll‑hoop, rear diffuser, or side‑pod). Keep a tight inventory so you can move it for optimal CG. |
| **You’re right at the limit** | Double‑check the scale calibration, driver weight, and fuel level. Small discrepancies (±1 kg) can be enough to trigger a penalty. |
| **Regulation change is upcoming** | Build a margin of flexibility into your design – e.g., modular rear‑subframe or adjustable bulkheads – so you can adapt to a ±5 kg shift without a full redesign. |
| **Weight‑saving vs. durability** | Remember that a lighter chassis often means thinner skin, which can affect crash‑worthiness and component life. FIA/CSCC scrutineers will inspect for structural integrity, not just weight. |
