

## Article 45 – Post Qualifying and Post Race Parc Fermé (New in 2026)

This article was **added entirely new** in the 2026 regulations and did not exist in the 2025 version. It establishes the rules governing the parc fermé area following qualifying sessions and races.

**Article 45.1** – Access restriction: Only officials charged with supervision may enter the parc fermé. No intervention of any kind is allowed unless authorised by such officials.

**Article 45.2** – Boundary definition: When the parc fermé is in use, parc fermé regulations apply in the area between the Line and the parc fermé entrance.

**Article 45.3** – Security: The parc fermé shall be secured such that no unauthorised persons can gain access to it.

**Article 45.4** – Team member for fuel cell: Competitors may appoint one team member to release pressure inside the fuel cell when cars return after each session and to attach fans to the car. This team member must request authorisation from the FIA Technical Delegate on site and is only authorised to perform these specific actions.

**Article 45.5** – Data download staff: Competitors may appoint one operational staff member to download data in parc fermé. This staff member must request authorisation from the FIA Technical Delegate on site and is only authorised to perform this action.

**Article 45.6** – Data submission: A complete copy of logged data must be submitted to the F3 Technical Director no later than one hour after the end of each track session or parc fermé period.

**Article 45.7** – Flash disc removal: The remote flash disc recorder located in the cockpit may be removed by the drivers at the end of qualifying and races.
