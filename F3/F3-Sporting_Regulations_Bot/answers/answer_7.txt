**Alternatives to a grid‑place penalty (as set out in the 2026 Formula 3 Sporting Regulations and the 2026 Penalty Guidelines)**  

| Alternative | Typical sanction | Where it appears |
|-------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Time penalty** (5 s, 10 s, 30 s …) | Added to the driver’s race time | Articles 21.5, 37.4, 40.5, 41.3‑41.5 (Penalty Guidelines) |
| **Stop‑and‑go penalty** (mandatory) | Car must stop in the pit lane for a set time (e.g., 10 s) before re‑joining | Articles 21.5, 24.1(g), 37.4, 40.5, 41.3‑41.5 |
| **Drive‑through penalty** | Car must drive through the pit lane at the limit speed without stopping | Articles 21.5, 24.2, 37.5, 40.5, 41.3‑41.5 |
| **Reprimand** (with or without a fine) | Formal warning; sometimes coupled with a €1 000/€2 000 fine | Articles 21.5 (Arm‑Band breach), 22.4, 22.9, 40.5 |
| **Disqualification** (from a session, race or event) | Driver/competitor excluded from the session, race or whole event; may include a €10 000 fine | Articles 21.5 (operational‑personnel breach), 39.3 (serious infractions), 45 (parc fé rme) |
| **Fine** ( monetary only) | Payment of a specified amount (e.g., €20 000 for testing breaches) | Articles 10.4 (testing), 21.5 (Arm‑Band), 45 (parc fé rme) |
| **Reduction of available tyres/planks** | Fewer tyres or planks may be allocated in subsequent events | Articles 24 (tyre allocation), 24.16 (plank limitation) |
| **Loss of championship points** | Points deducted from the driver’s or team’s tally | Article 39.3 (grid‑place penalty can be replaced by points loss in certain cases) |

**Key references**:  
- **Article 21.5** – Operational personnel breaches and associated penalties.  
- **Article 37.4 – 43.3** – Wheel‑fitting and start‑procedure penalties.  
- **Article 40.5** – Safety‑car related penalties (time, stop‑and‑go, drive‑through).  
- **Penalty Guidelines (2026, Version 1.0 – 5 Mar 2026)** – Tables listing sanctions for each offence (e.g., “5 s stop‑and‑go (mandatory)”, “Drive‑through”, “Reprimand”, “Disqualification”).  

These alternatives may be applied **instead of** a grid‑place penalty at the stewards’ discretion, depending on the nature and severity of the infringement.
