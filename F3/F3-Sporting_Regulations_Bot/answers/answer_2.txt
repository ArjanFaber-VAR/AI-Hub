**Summary of the most significant 2025 → 2026 changes (key articles)**  

- **Cover page / regulation identification** – 2025 Formula 3 Sporting Regulations 1/57 V2 26 Feb 2025 → 2026 Formula 3 Sporting Regulations 1/57 V1 10 Dec 2025 (overall regulation).  

- **Article 2 – General Undertaking** – reference to the **2026** International Sporting Code (instead of 2025).  

- **Article 4 – Licences** – only a formatting change (extra space before the period).  

- **Article 5 – Competition Calendar** – championship year changed to **2026**; publication deadline now “before 15 January 2026”; reference to the 2026 regulation version.  

- **Article 6.4 – Entry Limits** – entry limit wording now for the **2026** Championship.  

- **Article 6.5 – Points When Race Distance Is Reduced** – minor spacing change in “end ‑of‑session”.  

- **Article 8.2 – Competitors’ Applications** – competition‑number allocation now based on the **2025** Team Championship order (previously 2024).  

- **Article 10.2 – Testing** – wording changed from “any type of activity …” to “No driver … may carry out any type of activity …”.  

- **Article 17.3 – Appeals** – fee wording now “accompanied by a fee of €6000”.  

- **Article 26.2 – Driver limit** – permitted drivers per competitor increased from **five** to **seven**.  

- **Article 27.5 – Red‑flag safety‑car procedure** – new requirement for drivers to stay above the minimum ECU‑set time in each marshalling sector.  

- **Article 28.5 – Pit‑lane start** – wording changed to “unless a car is pushed from the grid … may only be driven …”.  

- **Article 28.6 – Qualifying‑practice pit‑position** – added “must do so without delay in the fast lane”.  

- **Article 28.14 – Start‑set‑up** – now permitted “to activate the Start set‑up procedure at any time the car is in the pit lane”.  

- **Article 29.1 – Weighing** – expanded penalty wording for failure to stop/return to the weighing area.  

- **Article 30.2 – Refuelling** – changed from “Refuelling is only permitted … Fuel may not be added …” to “Fuel may be added to or removed from a car only in the Competitors’ designated garages or parc ferme”.  

- **Article 33.3 – Qualifying groups (Monaco/Monza)** – group‑run times now differ (Monaco 16 min/Monza 10 min).  

- **Article 34.5 f – Grid allocation** – paragraph f removed entirely.  

- **Article 35.2 – Alternative Sprint‑race grid** – now allowed in **Monaco and Monza**.  

- **Article 36.2 – Feature‑race grid** – now allowed in **Monaco and Monza**; added clarification on grid‑order wording.  

- **Article 37.4‑37.6 – Starting procedure** – “wheels fully fitted”, “Start set‑up procedure activated” added to three‑minute, one‑minute and formation‑lap instructions.  

- **Article 39.3 – Grid‑place penalty** – “at their next Feature Race” (instead of “next Race”).  

- **Article 40 – Safety‑car** – updated description of safety‑car driver/observer and timing of safety‑car deployment.  

- **Article 41.3 & 41.8 – VSC** – minor wording corrections (“while” vs “whilst”, “whilst” → “while”).  

- **Article 42.5 – Fast‑lane handling** – paragraph revised; new work‑in‑fast‑lane list (i‑xi) added.  

- **Article 43.3 – Wheel‑fitting** – “must have their wheels **fully** fitted”.  

- **Article 43.5 – Start‑set‑up after pit‑lane work** – “Start from pit lane (mandatory)”.  

- **Article 44‑45 (new)** – added introductory paragraphs on plank serial‑number declaration and pit‑stop equipment.  

- **Appendix 3 – Driver’s Deed** – championship year updated to **2026** throughout.  

- **Penalty Guidelines (2026 version)** – header now includes “IMPORTANT NOTE: PENALTY POINTS ARE NOT PERMITTED TO BE IMPOSED BY THE STEWARDS IF THE PENALTY IS A REPRIMAND OR A FINE.”  

These changes are drawn directly from the comparative excerpts between the 2025 and 2026 Formula 3 Sporting Regulations and associated documents.
